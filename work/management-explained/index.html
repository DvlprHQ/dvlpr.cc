The brief

Management books are long. Most leadership advice online is vague, generic, or written by consultants who haven't managed anyone in a decade. First-time managers get no useful structured guidance.

Management Explained is the distilled version — frameworks explained simply, with context on when to use them and why they work. Think of it as the cheat sheet for becoming a better manager without reading 10 books.

No theory for the sake of theory. Every framework on the site has a one-line summary of when to use it and a real example of it working in practice.

What it covers

OKRs and goal-setting frameworks, RACI matrices and accountability structures, feedback loops and 1:1 structures, skip-level meetings, onboarding playbooks, performance management, and delegation models.

Each article is structured the same way: what is it, when does it work, when does it fail, and a worked example. Readers can scan or go deep.

Who it's for

First-time managers figuring out how to structure their work. Mid-level leaders levelling up. Individual contributors who want to understand how management decisions get made around them.

Free to read. No paywall, no newsletter gate, no account required.

Technical approach

Built with Next.js and MDX so content is just markdown files — no CMS, no database, no vendor lock-in. Deployed to Vercel. Pages load in under 400ms globally. The whole site is a static export.
