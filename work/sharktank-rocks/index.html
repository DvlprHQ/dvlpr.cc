The brief

SharkTank Rocks started as a side project born out of frustration: too much startup content online is either cheerleading or pile-ons. Neither is useful to founders trying to improve.

The site publishes detailed breakdowns of real startup pitches and investor decks — what worked, what didn't, and what the founder should have done differently. No hype, no dunking. Just honest craft critique.

Think of it like a film critic's column for startup pitches. The goal isn't to be harsh — it's to be useful. Most feedback founders get is either too vague or too personal.

Editorial approach

Each breakdown follows a consistent structure: context about the company and funding stage, the pitch or deck in full, then a section-by-section critique with a specific recommendation for each problem identified.

Nothing is published without a concrete "here's what I would have done instead." Pointing at problems without offering alternatives isn't useful.

Design

Editorial-first layout. Long-form content deserves long-form thinking on the design side: wide measure, generous line-height, strong typographic hierarchy. No sidebars, no recommended articles mid-read. Just the content.

Dark mode by default because most founders read this stuff late at night.
